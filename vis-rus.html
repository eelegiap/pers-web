Visualizing Russian

Visualizing Russian is a suite of web-based tools for Russian language learners, researchers, and teachers. It is an ongoing project that I am working on along with Dr. Steven Clancy, Director of the Language Program in the Department of Slavic Languages & Literatures at Harvard.

My role in the project has been to design and implement new tools to add to the site. So far, I've created working (final?) versions of two tools: Quick Lemma and Case Distribution.

Quick Lemma

The purpose of Quick Lemma is to return the frequencies of different words to the user (and a visualization of frequencies changing over time). There are four different buttons that return slightly different results. Note: lemma refers to the dictionary (nominative) form of a word, while form refers to its grammatical inflection.

  • Get Lemma Frequencies: returns the frequencies of the lemmas (regardless of form)
  • Get Form Frequencies: returns the frequencies of distinct word forms / inflections
